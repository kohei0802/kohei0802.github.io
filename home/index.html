Yakushima

Yakushima is a Japanese legend. This tiny island is the country's first UNESCO World Natural Heritage site, inspired the Studio Ghibli film Princess Mononoke, and is home to some of Japan's top natural wonders.

Recommendation

  • Waterfalls — Yakushima's wet climate and rugged terrain mean that there are an abundance of huge waterfalls.
  • Ancient Forests — There are Yakusugi and ancient cedar trees on Yakushima. The oldest tree is said to be 7,000 years old.
  • Sea Turtle Season - From May to July each year, Nagata Inakahama, a beach, is the largest nesting grounds for sea turtles in Japan.

Access

Just 60 kilometers off the tip of Kagoshima, Yakushima is accessible by plane or ferry.

From Tokyo, take a plane from Haneda Airport to Kagoshima Airport. By plane, it takes less than two hours. From there, if you take a connecting flight to Yakushima, it takes about 40 minutes. Another option is to take a ferry from Kagoshima Port to Yakushima Miyanoura Port or Anbo Port. Ferries take 2.5 hours by fast boat, or 4 hours by regular ferry, and cheaper by car ferry.

It's good to be remote

Compared to other parts of Japan, Yakushima remains relatively untouched because it is not easily accessible. In 1993, the island was inscribed as a UNESCO World Natural Heritage site. The pristine coastline and crystal clear waters attract tourists from all over Japan. Yet remoteness means the island is never too touristy.

Yakushima, together with the Shirakami Mountains in Tohoku, is the first place in Japan to be inscribed on the list of UNESCO World Natural Heritage Sites.

About 90 percent of the island is forested mountains, with its highest point being the summit of Mt. Miyanoura, which is the highest mountain in Kyushu at 1,936 meters above sea level. Miyanouradake is located in the center of the island and is surrounded by many other mountains over 1,500 meters high.

Varieties of Nature

From the island's sandy beaches to the tops of its highest mountains, Yakushima has a variety of microclimates ranging from subtropical to subarctic. This variation has resulted in a staggering biodiversity. Due to the high rainfall throughout the year, the island is covered with a green forest of Yakusugi cedar trees (ancient cedar trees) that are over 1,000 years old.

Notable tourist destinations include Shiratani Unsuikyo, Yakusugi Land, and Nagata Inaka Beach. Shiratani Unsuikyo is filled with beautiful valleys and primeval forests, while Yakusugi Land has a vast collection of Yakusugi cedar trees. Nagata Inakahama is the largest sea turtle nesting area in Japan.
